Deoxyribonucleic acid (abbreviated DNA) is the molecule that carries genetic information for the development and functioning of an organism. In all living things, DNA is essential for inheritance, coding for proteins, and providing instructions for life and its processes. Scientists can use DNA technology to compare the DNA of cells in a disease state to those of a healthy state. This can help them understand the changes that occur during disease progression and develop treatments. DNA technology can also be used to create new medicine and treatment for disease, such as vaccines.
